<p>This article contains a list of the most studied restriction enzymes whose names start with Bsa to Bso inclusive. It contains approximately 90 enzymes.
The following information is given:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Enzyme</b>: Accepted name of the <a href="/wiki/Molecule" title="Molecule">molecule</a>, according to the internationally adopted <a href="/wiki/Nomenclature" title="Nomenclature">nomenclature</a><sup id="cite_ref-pmid4588280_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid4588280-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pmid12654995_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid12654995-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>, and <a href="/wiki/Reference" title="Reference">bibliographical references</a>. <i>(Further reading: see the section "<a href="/wiki/Restriction_enzyme#Nomenclature" title="Restriction enzyme">Nomenclature</a>" in the article "<a href="/wiki/Restriction_enzyme" title="Restriction enzyme">Restriction enzyme</a>".)</i></li>
<li><b>PDB code</b>: Code used to identify the structure of a protein in the <a href="/wiki/Protein_Data_Bank" title="Protein Data Bank">PDB</a> database of protein structures. The 3D atomic structure of a protein provides highly valuable information to understand the intimate details of its mechanism of action<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>.</li>
<li><b>Source</b>: Organism that naturally produces the enzyme.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Recognition_sequence" title="Recognition sequence">Recognition sequence</a></b>: Sequence of DNA recognized by the enzyme and to which it specifically binds.</li>
<li><b>Cut</b>: Cutting site and DNA products of the cut. The <a href="/wiki/Recognition_sequence" title="Recognition sequence">recognition sequence</a> and the cutting site usually match, but sometimes the cutting site can be dozens of nucleotides away from the recognition site<sup id="cite_ref-pmid2172084_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid2172084-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pmid11557805_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid11557805-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>.</li>
<li><b>Isoschizomers and neoschizomers</b>: An <a href="/wiki/Isoschizomer" title="Isoschizomer">isoschizomer</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">enzyme</a> that recognizes the same sequence as another. A <a href="/wiki/Neoschizomer" title="Neoschizomer">neoschizomer</a> is a special type of isoschizomer that recognizes the same sequence as another, but cuts in a different manner. A maximum number of 8-10 most common isoschizomers are indicated for every enzyme but there may be many more. Neoschizomers are shown in bold and green color font (e.g.: <span style="color:#4CBB17"><b>BamHI</b></span>). When "None on <i>date</i>" is indicated, that means that there were no registered isoschizomers in the databases on that date with a clearly defined cutting site. Isoschizomers indicated in white font and grey background correspond to enzymes not listed in the current lists:</li></ul>
