</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Restriction_enzyme" title="Restriction enzyme">restriction enzyme</a></div>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/Homing_endonuclease" title="Homing endonuclease">Homing endonuclease</a></div>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Restriction_enzyme" title="Restriction enzyme">restriction enzyme</a> or restriction <a href="/wiki/Endonuclease" title="Endonuclease">endonuclease</a> is a special type of biological <a href="/wiki/Macromolecule" title="Macromolecule">macromolecule</a> that functions as part of the "<a href="/wiki/Immune_system" title="Immune system">immune system</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">bacteria</a>. One special kind of restriction enzymes is the class of "<a href="/wiki/Homing_endonucleases" class="mw-redirect" title="Homing endonucleases">homing endonucleases</a>", these being present in all <a href="/wiki/Three-domain_system" title="Three-domain system">three domains</a> of life, although their function seems to be very different from one domain to another.
</p><p>The classical restriction enzymes cut up, and hence render harmless, any unknown (non-<a href="/wiki/Cell_(biology)" title="Cell (biology)">cellular</a>) <a href="/wiki/DNA" title="DNA">DNA</a> that enters a bacterial cell as a result of a <a href="/wiki/Bacteriophage" title="Bacteriophage">viral infection</a>. They recognize a specific DNA sequence, usually short (3 to 8 <a href="/wiki/Base_pair" title="Base pair">bp</a>), and cut it, producing either blunt or overhung ends, either at or nearby the <a href="/wiki/Recognition_sequence" title="Recognition sequence">recognition site</a>.
</p><p>Restriction enzymes are quite variable in the short DNA sequences they recognize. An organism often has several different enzymes, each specific to a distinct short DNA sequence.<sup id="cite_ref-pmid6243774_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid6243774-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Restriction_enzymes_catalog">Restriction enzymes catalog</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=List_of_restriction_enzyme_cutting_sites&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Restriction enzymes catalog"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The list includes some of the most studied examples of <a href="/wiki/Restriction_enzymes" class="mw-redirect" title="Restriction enzymes">restriction endoncleases</a>. The following information is given:
</p>
<ul><li><b>Enzyme</b>: Accepted name of the <a href="/wiki/Molecule" title="Molecule">molecule</a>, according to the internationally adopted <a href="/wiki/Nomenclature" title="Nomenclature">nomenclature</a><sup id="cite_ref-pmid4588280_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid4588280-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pmid12654995_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid12654995-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>, and <a href="/wiki/Reference" title="Reference">bibliographical references</a>. <i>(Further reading: see the section "<a href="/wiki/Restriction_enzyme#Nomenclature" title="Restriction enzyme">Nomenclature</a>" in the article "<a href="/wiki/Restriction_enzyme" title="Restriction enzyme">Restriction enzyme</a>".)</i></li>
<li><b>PDB code</b>: Code used to identify the structure of a protein in the <a href="/wiki/Protein_Data_Bank" title="Protein Data Bank">PDB</a> database of protein structures. The 3D atomic structure of a protein provides highly valuable information to understand the intimate details of its mechanism of action<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>.</li>
<li><b>Source</b>: Organism that naturally produces the enzyme.</li>
<li><b><a href="/wiki/Recognition_sequence" title="Recognition sequence">Recognition sequence</a></b>: Sequence of DNA recognized by the enzyme and to which it specifically binds.</li>
<li><b>Cut</b>: Cutting site and DNA products of the cut. The <a href="/wiki/Recognition_sequence" title="Recognition sequence">recognition sequence</a> and the cutting site usually match, but sometimes the cutting site can be dozens of nucleotides away from the recognition site<sup id="cite_ref-pmid2172084_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid2172084-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pmid11557805_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pmid11557805-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>.</li>
<li><b>Isoschizomers and neoschizomers</b>: An <a href="/wiki/Isoschizomer" title="Isoschizomer">isoschizomer</a> is an <a href="/wiki/Enzyme" title="Enzyme">enzyme</a> that recognizes the same sequence as another. A <a href="/wiki/Neoschizomer" title="Neoschizomer">neoschizomer</a> is a special type of isoschizomer that recognizes the same sequence as another, but cuts in a different manner. A maximum number of 8-10 most common isoschizomers are indicated for every enzyme but there may be many more. Neoschizomers are shown in bold and green color font (e.g.: <span style="color:#4CBB17"><b>BamHI</b></span>). When "None on <i>date</i>" is indicated, that means that there were no registered isoschizomers in the databases on that date with a clearly defined cutting site. Isoschizomers indicated in white font and grey background correspond to enzymes not listed in the current lists:</li></ul>

<tbody><tr>

</td>
<td style="background: darkgray; color: white"><span style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#160;</span>EcoR70I<span style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#160;</span>
</td></tr></tbody></table></dd></dl><p>
